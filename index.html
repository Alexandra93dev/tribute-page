Frank Sinatra

Frank Sinatra performing
  • 1915 - Born in Hoboken, New Jersey, on December 12
  • 1939 - Married childhood sweetheart Nancy Barbato. They had three children before their marriage unraveled in the late 1940s
  • 1940 - Tommy Dorsey invited Sinatra to join his band. After two years Sinatra decided to strike out on his own.
  • 1943-1946 - Sinatra's solo career blossomed as a singer. His fans called him The Voice and The Sultan of Swoon.
  • 1943 - Sinatra made his movie acting debut in with the films Reveille With Beverley and Higher and Higher.
  • early 1950s - Sinatra's popularity began to slide in the postwar years, leading to a loss of his recording and film contracts
  • 1951 - Sinatra married actress Ava Gardner but they split afterwards
  • 1953 - He made a triumphant comeback, winning a supporting actor Oscar for his portrayal of the Italian-American soldier Maggio in the classic From Here to Eternity
  • 1955 - He received another Academy Award nomination for his work in The Man with the Golden Arm
  • 1965 - He received a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award and headlined the Newport Jazz Festival with Count Basie's Orchestra.
  • 1966 - Sinatra remarried a third time, to Mia Farrow, in. But that union, too, ended in divorce.
  • 1966 - Sinatra had a big hit in with the Billboard No. 1 track "Strangers in the Night," which won a Grammy for record of the year.
  • 1967 - Sinatra and daughter Nancy reached No. 1 for four weeks with "Something Stupid". By the end of the decade, Sinatra had added another signature song to his repertory - My Way
  • 1973 - After a brief retirement Sinatra returned to the music scene with the album Ol' Blue Eyes Is Back
  • 1976 - Sinatra married for a fourth and final time in to Barbara Blakely Marx
  • 1993 - He gained legions of new fans with the release of Duets, a collection of 13 Sinatra standards that he rerecorded, featuring the likes of Barbra Streisand, Bono, Tony Bennett and Aretha Franklin
  • 1995 - performed in concert for the last time in at the Palm Desert Marriott Ballroom in California
  • 1998 - Died on May 14 of a heart attack at Los Angeles' Cedars-Sinai Medical Center. He was 82 years old
